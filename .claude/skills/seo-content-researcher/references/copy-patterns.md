# Copy Patterns — Editorial Sentence Structures

Use these as scaffolding, not fill-in-the-blank templates. Always rewrite for specificity.

## Homepage hero

**Eyebrow pattern:** `The <Collection/Edit> · <Year>` · `Festive <Year>` · `New Arrivals` · `Heirlooms Reimagined`

**H1 patterns**
- `<Emotive noun>, <modifier>.` — e.g. "Heirlooms, styled for today."
- `<Craft>. <Occasion>. <Promise>.` — e.g. "Handloom. Festive. Ready for the modern wardrobe."
- `For the women who wear their stories.`
- `Where tradition meets the modern wardrobe.`

**Lede pattern**
> A premium boutique of <primary category list> — <craft word> and <finish word> with care for <audience>.

## Category landing intros

**Pattern A — craft-first**
> Every <category> in this edit is <craft qualifier>, finished in <fabric/technique>, and styled for <occasion range>. From <soft example> to <dramatic example>, each piece carries the weight of the women who wear it.

**Pattern B — occasion-first**
> Whether you are dressing for <occasion 1>, <occasion 2>, or the quiet ceremony of <occasion 3>, our <category> collection meets the moment. <Craft sentence>. <Styling sentence>.

**Pattern C — heritage-first**
> Rooted in <region/tradition>, reimagined for the modern wardrobe. Our <category> collection honours <specific craft> while <modern promise>.

## PDP opener (1–2 sentences)

- `A <craft adjective> <category> in <fabric>, finished with <signature detail>.`
- `Hand-<technique> on <fabric>, this <category> carries the kind of quiet luxury that makes an entrance of its own.`
- `The kind of <category> you return to — for <occasion 1>, <occasion 2>, and the evenings in between.`

## PDP body blocks

**Fabric & craft**
> Woven in <fabric>, this piece is <technique>-embroidered by hand. The <detail> is worked in <material>, with <border/pallu/neckline> framed in <contrast detail>.

**Styling notes**
> Pair with <accessory cue 1> for <occasion 1>, or let the <signature detail> carry the look with <accessory cue 2>.

**Care & origin**
> <Fabric>, finished in India. Dry clean only. [VERIFY: region of origin, artisan credit]

## Meta title patterns (≤ 60 chars)

- `<Primary keyword> | Sakthi Trends USA` — e.g. `Hand-Embroidered Silk Sarees | Sakthi Trends USA` (48)
- `<Primary keyword> for <Occasion> | Sakthi Trends USA`
- `Shop <Category>: <Hook> | Sakthi Trends USA`

## Meta description patterns (≤ 155 chars)

- `Discover <primary keyword> from Sakthi Trends USA — <craft descriptor>, <occasion descriptor>. <Soft CTA>.`
- `<Primary keyword> hand-finished for the modern wardrobe. <Secondary keyword>. Shipped across the US.`

Always run a character count. Trim before committing.

## Open Graph description patterns

More editorial than meta description — up to ~200 chars, can lead with emotion rather than keywords.
> Heirlooms for the women who wear their stories. Hand-embroidered sarees, kurthis, and lehengas — a premium boutique edit from Sakthi Trends USA.

## FAQ question patterns (match real search intent)

- `What is a <fabric/craft> saree?`
- `How do I care for a <fabric> saree?`
- `What occasion is a <category> suitable for?`
- `Do you ship internationally?`
- `How do I choose the right size for <category>?`
- `What is the difference between <item A> and <item B>?`

Answers: 2–4 sentences. Informative, not promotional. Include 1 primary keyword naturally per answer.

## Tonal dos and don'ts

**Voice**: editorial · cultural · warm · confident · restrained
**Reading level**: adult, literate, unhurried — think Vogue India, not discount marketplace

**Do say**: "finished by hand" · "quietly luxurious" · "made for occasion and the everyday" · "rooted in tradition"
**Don't say**: "best deals" · "amazing collection" · "shop now and save" · "huge discount" · "unbeatable prices"

**Do pair**: craft + occasion + fabric in the same sentence — dense meaning, not dense keywords
**Don't pair**: three keywords in a row with no connective tissue
